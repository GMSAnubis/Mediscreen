Welcome to Mediscreen

When you writes notes for your patients keep in mind that these words are use as triggers to calculate diabetes risks. They are only counted one, even if you write them multiple times, to avoid false results.

For example if you write "patient is below height" or "patient height is below normal height", the trigger word heigh is counted one time only, no matter if it's over or below, and no matter how many times this word is written in one or multiple notes. Triggers words are not case sensitive

Triggers words:
  • Hemoglobin A1C
  • MicroAlbumin
  • Height
  • Weight
  • Smoker
  • Abnormal
  • Cholesterol
  • Dizziness
  • Relapse
  • Reaction
  • Antibodies

If you want to add more triggers words, or modify the risk calculation process, feel free to contact us.
